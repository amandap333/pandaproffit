As a new parent you are always wondering if your baby is okay. You are worrying if your friends baby reaches a milestone before yours or if you are doing everything you can to help your baby learn. Here is some information about what milestones your baby should be reaching for the first year, and how you can help your baby flurish.

2 Month Milestones

  1. Can briefly calm themself
  2. Begins to smile at people
  3. Tries to look at parent or caregiver
  4. Coos, makes gugling sounds
  5. Turns head toward sounds
  6. Pays attnetion to faces
  7. Begins to act bored

How can you help?

  1. Cuddle, talk and play with your baby during feeding, dressing, and bathing.
  2. Help your baby learn to calm herself, put babys hand in mouth to help soothe.
  3. Begin to help your baby get into a routine
  4. Act excited and smile when your baby makes sounds
  5. Talk, read, and sing to your baby

4 Month Milestones

  1. Smiles spontaneously
  2. Begins to babble
  3. Likes to play
  4. cries in different ways

How can you help?

  1. Copy your babys sounds
  2. Play games such as peek-a-boo
  3. Put toys near your baby so that she can reach for them

Always allow enough time for your baby to have tummy time every day. Tummy time is crucial. Since the back to sleep campaign in 1994, babies don't spend as much time on their tummies to strengthen those muscles. Allowing tummy time every day will make your baby happier overall because it will strengthen those muscles allowing your baby to reach those milestones!
